# Atlas Industries — Code of Conduct

**Policy ID:** HR-COC-004
**Owner:** Legal & People Operations
**Effective Date:** 15 March 2024
**Version:** 2.1

---

## 1. Our Commitment

Atlas Industries is committed to a workplace built on integrity, mutual respect, and accountability. Every employee, contractor, and board member is expected to uphold this code regardless of role or tenure.

## 2. Professional Conduct

### 2.1 Respect & Inclusion
- Discrimination on the basis of gender, religion, ethnicity, nationality, age, disability, or sexual orientation is strictly prohibited.
- Harassment of any form — verbal, written, physical, or digital — will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 2.2 Dress Code
- Business casual is the default for office days.
- Client-facing meetings require business formal attire.
- Engineering teams may dress casually on internal-only days.

## 3. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose, in writing to their manager and HR within 30 days, any of the following:
- A second paid role outside Atlas Industries
- A personal financial interest in a current or prospective vendor or customer
- A family relationship with another Atlas employee, contractor, or vendor representative

Failure to disclose constitutes a serious breach and may result in termination.

## 4. Confidentiality

- All non-public business information is confidential and remains the property of Atlas Industries.
- Sharing internal documents, financials, client data, or source code outside Atlas without written authorization is grounds for immediate dismissal and legal action.
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment for 3 years.

## 5. Use of Company Resources

- Company laptops, phones, and accounts are for work use. Limited personal use is tolerated but must not interfere with duties.
- See IT-AUP-006 (Acceptable Use Policy) for technical details.

## 6. Anti-Bribery & Anti-Corruption

- No employee may offer, accept, solicit, or facilitate any bribe, kickback, or improper payment.
- Gifts received from vendors or clients exceeding **500 EGP** in value must be declared to Legal within 5 business days.
- Hospitality (meals, events) is acceptable when reasonable and disclosed if exceeding the gift threshold.

## 7. Reporting Violations

- Employees may report suspected violations through:
  - Their direct manager
  - HR Business Partner
  - The anonymous ethics hotline: **ethics@atlas-industries.com**
- Retaliation against good-faith reporters is itself a violation of this code.

## 8. Enforcement

Violations are investigated by HR and, where appropriate, Legal. Outcomes range from written warning to termination, depending on severity and recurrence. Documented patterns of minor violations may compound to a serious disciplinary outcome.
